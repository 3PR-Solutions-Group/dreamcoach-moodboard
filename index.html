Brand Guidelines
DO
  • Use uppercase + letter-spacing for all section labels
  • Emerald as the sole meaningful accent colour
  • Whitespace as active design element — give content room
  • Gem/diamond as the iconographic foundation
  • Dark borders instead of shadows for depth
  • Off-white text, never pure white (reduces eye strain)
DON'T
  • Add gradients to backgrounds — keep surfaces flat
  • Use more than 2 accent colours in the same view
  • Round corners beyond 8px — it signals "friendly fintech"
  • Add decorative illustrations or mascots
  • Use Inter below 12px — switch to DM Mono for micro-labels
  • Break the uppercase label rule for sub-headings
Why This Direction
01
Dark-first is genuinely rare in wellnessOnly 20% of the personal growth app market uses a dark-first aesthetic — and growing. DreamCoach claims a visually uncrowded position while signalling premium to the target audience.
02
Emerald + dark is psychologically groundedEmerald signals growth (botanical associations), clarity (gem clarity), and premium (luxury brand palettes). Dark base signals seriousness and reduces eye strain for evening check-ins — a key accountability moment.
03
David has already invested in this directionThe existing prototype demonstrates clear aesthetic intent. Validating and elevating that direction reduces revision cycles and delivers fastest to market. Zero risk of "this isn't what I wanted."
What Dave Changes vs. David's Prototype
→Typography hierarchy clarity. More weight variation (700/600/500/400) for clearer hierarchy without adding more sizes.
→Component consistency. Buttons, cards, inputs follow a consistent padding and radius logic. No one-offs.
→Accessibility verification. Every colour combination tested against WCAG AA. Emerald on deep navy may not meet spec in the prototype.
→Icon system consistency. The gem motif systematised — every status indicator uses a gem variant. Currently ad hoc in prototype.
→4px spacing system. 4, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32 base unit. Consistent padding throughout eliminates visual inconsistency.
Choose Direction A if…
You want your design vision validated and elevated — not replaced. This is the direction for founders who knew what they wanted from the start.
Speed to market matters. No re-brief, no visual pivot, no "let me think about it" — just production-quality execution of your existing direction.
You believe the dark-emerald aesthetic is the strongest market position — and the research in this document confirms you're right.

Mood

"Dark-first premium meets intentional warmth. The sapphire insight cards say: this app is thinking about you. The amber team updates say: your people are here. The gold milestones say: this moment matters. Three accent colours, three emotional registers — all in service of the same thing."

Brand Guidelines
DO
  • Sapphire for psychological insight content exclusively
  • Warm amber for all team and social moments
  • Serif headlines for emotional and aspirational copy
  • Motion on all milestone moments — streak, team, letting go
  • Colour-code card top borders by content type
DON'T
  • Mix accent colours within a single card
  • Use serif fonts for UI labels or navigation
  • Add motion to structural elements (nav, cards) — reserve for moments
  • Use amber for anything except social/team content
Why This Direction Wins
01
Serif + sans-serif pairing is the premium standardEvery luxury app (Apple, Stripe, high-end fintech) uses this combination. It signals professional credibility while the dark base maintains visual identity.
02
Multi-colour system creates user intelligenceUsers learn: sapphire = insight, amber = social. This reduces cognitive load while increasing delight. The colour becomes the communication.
03
Motion design is a market differentiatorMost wellness apps have no motion design whatsoever. DreamCoach ships motion-first — streak animations, team feed reveals, letting go transitions — and immediately feels more premium than competitors.
Choose Direction B if…
You want "yes, and" — the visual DNA you built, elevated with the professional details that separate good apps from great ones.
You've looked at Linear or Stripe and thought "that's the energy I want" — because that's what the serif headlines and sapphire accents deliver.
You want the app to feel like it cares about the user — because motion design and colour semantics create that emotional connection without a single word of copy.

For the Gallery Owner

David curates art. His eye is trained to notice composition, balance, negative space, and intentionality. Direction C is the direction designed for that eye — not for the habit tracker market, but for a founder who will look at his own product every day and need it to be something he's genuinely proud of. The serif headings, the warm palette, the editorial spacing — these are the choices of someone who takes aesthetics seriously.

Reference Products
Aesop
Warm palette, considered typography, nothing superfluous. Every surface is a decision. Study: how they use whitespace as the primary design element.
Are.na
Editorial serenity. Sophisticated content platform that trusts its users. Study: how they use typography to signal intelligence without decoration.
The Gentlewoman
Cream + forest green is their signature. Editorial typography at maximum sophistication. Study: how they treat every page as a poster.
Brand Guidelines
DO
  • Generous whitespace — more than feels comfortable
  • Serif for all headlines, Inter for all UI and labels
  • Terracotta for the Letting Go pathway exclusively
  • Forest green as the primary action colour
  • Grain texture (2% opacity) on backgrounds
  • Editorial image sizes — full-bleed or nothing
DON'T
  • Use more than 3 colours in any single view
  • Add dark surfaces to light screens — maintain the warmth
  • Use sharp edges — 12px radius minimum
  • Round corners beyond 16px on cards — it becomes soft fintech
  • Use the gold accent decoratively — ceremony and milestones only
Why This Direction
01
Warm in a cool market is a genuine differentiatorEvery dark app feels cold. Every pastel app feels weak. Cream + forest green + warm serif is the only aesthetic that is both sophisticated and warm — a position no competitor currently occupies.
02
Editorial sophistication signals the brand David is buildingDreamCoach isn't a habit app. It's a psychological operating system. The design should reflect that ambition — and editorial typography + warm restraint communicates depth that dark apps can't quite reach.
03
Ceremonial framing is uniquely appropriate for the Letting Go featureA release ceremony deserves a ceremonial aesthetic. When the user arrives at the Letting Go pathway, the warm terracotta background signals: this is different, this matters, this is not just another screen.
